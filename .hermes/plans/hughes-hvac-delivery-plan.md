# Hughes HVAC website delivery plan

Reading this as: industrial B2B redesign for offshore, oil and gas, marine and procurement audiences, with a robust engineering language leaning toward IBM Carbon structure, BMW-like photographic confidence and a restrained industrial brutalist edge.

Design dials: variance 5, motion 3, density 6. This audience needs confidence, legibility and technical detail more than spectacle.

## Sitemap
- Home: proposition, service summary, mission, engineering proof points and quick contact.
- About: 2014 formation, mission, values, leadership placeholders and company details.
- Services: seven service sections from the research file.
- Engineering services and project management: survey, design, procurement, fabrication, installation and commissioning flow.
- Projects: placeholder case study gallery for future client-supplied stories.
- Certifications and memberships: BSRIA, COSHH, PFEER/TR and testing compliance context.
- Contact: phone, address, hours, static enquiry form and map placeholder.
- Careers: optional recruitment page with placeholder opening.

## Visual directions considered
1. Dark engineering command: deep navy, off-white text, sharp panels, large offshore imagery. Strong credibility, but potentially heavy for procurement users scanning information.
2. Light technical dossier: off-white background, navy and green accents, gridded layouts, large documentary imagery, crisp technical type. Best balance of trust, readability and industrial feel.

Chosen direction: light technical dossier with dark navy sections used only as contained panels, not full theme inversion. It avoids startup gradients and keeps the company grounded in engineering.

## Acceptance gates
- Content remains sourced from the research file or marked as placeholder.
- Components read structured JSON content rather than embedding long copy in route components.
- DESIGN.md defines tokens and rules.
- Build, typecheck, lint, content tests and accessibility smoke checks pass.
- Browser visual QA confirms the site reads as an engineering firm, not a SaaS template.
